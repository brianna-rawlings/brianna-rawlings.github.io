About Me

I am a Computer Science and Digital Media student at Taylor University with a minor in Data Science. I’m passionate about building data-driven solutions that blend technology, sports, and human performance.

As an athlete and captain of the Women’s Soccer Team, I bring firsthand experience in sports performance and recovery, fueling my drive to innovate in wearable tech and sports analytics. Recently, I built an interactive WHOOP dashboard that explores how sleep, strain, and HRV affect recovery and performance; combining data science, machine learning, and interactive visualization.

My technical toolkit includes Python, R, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Django, and frameworks like Streamlit and Jupyter Notebooks. I’m also experienced in full-stack development, web design, and digital media. These skills I developed through coursework and my role as a Jr. Course Designer at Taylor University Online.

This summer, I’m interning at Ardalyst on an AI-driven project focused on automating federal proposal analysis and response generation. The work involves benchmarking top large language models (including OpenAI’s GPT-4), prototyping secure AI workflows using low-code tools, and addressing data security challenges in AI systems. This role is sharpening my skills in prompt engineering, API automation, and security-first AI design.

When I’m not coding or on the field, you’ll find me outdoors fishing, hiking, or camping and I love country music and ice cream!
